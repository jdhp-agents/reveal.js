Population methods

Descent methods:

  • A single solution is updated incrementally toward a local minimum

Population methods:

  • Iterative methods where several solutions (called individuals) are tested at each iteration
  • Information at different points in the solution space can be shared between individuals
  • Help the algorithm to avoid being stuck in a local optimum
  • Easy to parallelize

Evolutionary Algorithms

Let's start with Evolutionary Algorithms. This is one of the main family of population methods.

Evolutionary Algorithms

Biomimetic algorithms, inspired by natural selection and evolution


Fitter individuals are more likely to pass on their genes to the next generation.

Chromosomes of the fitter individuals are passed on to the next generation after undergoing the genetic operations of crossover and mutation


Evolution by Natural Selection Source: Khan Academy

Evolutionary algorithms are biomimetic algorithms, inspired by natural selection and evolution It reproduces the natural selection in silico. The main idea is the following : “Good” individuals (for a given problem : here survive and reproduce) are more likely to pass on their genes to the next generation. Chromosomes of these “good” individuals are passed on to the next generation after undergoing the genetic operations of crossover and mutation to give a chance for next generations individuals to be even better. In this illustration [...], we have mice of different colors. In this environment where rocks are dark, the dark mice are more likely to survive and reproduce as predators have more difficulties to see them. The color of the mice is a kind of “gene” that is passed on to the next generation. So the population of mice will evolve to be darker and darker to adapt to the environment.

Families of Evolutionary Algorithms (2/3)

Genetic Algorithms (GA)

  • J.H. Holland [1975]
  • Dissociate genotype and phenotype

The second family is probably the most famous, it’s genetic algorithms. Here the bio inspiration is even stronger as not only a kind of natural selection is reproduced but also genetic consideration. In ES we only have a phenotype. Here we also have a genotype and solutions are coded with genes. This has impacts on crossover and mutation operators, we will discuss that later.

Families of Evolutionary Algorithms (3/3)

Genetic Programming (GP)

  • J. Koza [1988]
  • Based on Genetic Algorithms, but individuals are programs

The third family of EA is less known ; it’s genetic programming and here solutions are coded as programs (with a tree of operators). We won’t discuss this family in details here but it can be interesting for some applications.
