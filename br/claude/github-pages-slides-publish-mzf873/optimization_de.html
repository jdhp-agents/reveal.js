Arbitrarily initialize the population

FOR EACH generation
$\quad$ FOR EACH children
$\quad\quad$ 1. MUTATION
$\quad\quad\quad$ Choose 3 random distinct individuals $\boldsymbol{a}$, $\boldsymbol{b}$ et $\boldsymbol{c}$
$\quad\quad\quad$ $\boldsymbol{b}$ and $\boldsymbol{c}$ define the direction and the amplitude of the mutation (i.e. the search vector)

$\quad\quad\quad$ Combine them to make an interim individual $\boldsymbol{z} \leftarrow \boldsymbol{a} + \omega ~ (\boldsymbol{b} - \boldsymbol{c})$
$\quad\quad\quad$ where $\omega$ is a mutation amplification factor (a scalar)

$\quad\quad$ 2. CROSSOVER
$\quad\quad\quad$ Choose a random dimension $j \in [1,\dots,n]$ for optimization in $n$ dimensions
$\quad\quad\quad$ Make the candidate individual $\boldsymbol{a}'$ using the binary crossover $$ a'_i = \begin{cases} z_i & \text{if } i = j \text{ or with probability } p \\ x_i & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$
$\quad\quad$ 3. SELECTION
$\quad\quad\quad$ Insert the best individual between $\boldsymbol{a}'$ and $\boldsymbol{a}$ into the next generation

RETURN the best individual in the last generation
